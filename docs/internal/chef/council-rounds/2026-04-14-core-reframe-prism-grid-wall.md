1. Is the CORE reframe (layered on the earlier "QUBi is the

## Skeptic (−1) — verdict: WARN, allow Option C with preconditions

**Top failure modes of the CORE reframe:**

1. **Immutability becomes avoidance.** The yearly cadence
   sounds disciplined until 2026-07-06 hits and there's a
   critical vulnerability, or a household member begs for a
   feature that "just takes one commit." The frame has no
   escape hatch. Real discipline means principled exceptions
   named in advance, not discovered in crisis.

2. **"Minimal" is undefined.** 29 commits on tonight's
   lifecycle is not minimal by any quantitative measure. The
   frame renames the problem without defining the threshold.
   Is minimal 5 commits? 10? 50? Until you answer, "minimal"
   is a feeling.

3. **The trinity is concentric, not modular.** If PRISM (the
   epistemology) breaks mid-year because a model gets
   poisoned, you cannot fix PRISM without touching GRID (the
   wiring that feeds it) and WALL (the audit that should have
   caught it). Calling them three separable "minimal updates"
   masks structural entanglement.

4. **Household appetite will not respect 2026-07-07.** Kids
   want features. Tonya notices UX bugs. If CORE is truly
   frozen, you're telling the household "no" for months. The
   frame will not survive contact with real family life.

5. **"Good for humanity" is a claim, not an engineering
   property.** The CORE reframe makes it *harder to break*,
   not *impossible to break*. And the frame does not account
   for the household discovering it was wrong about what
   "good" looks like.

**"Minimal" is undefined — unpacked:** the Chair has conflated
legitimacy and minimalism. 29 commits of tonight's work are
legitimate engineering. They are not minimal. The reframe is
letting the household avoid asking the real question: *What is
the actual threshold for CORE updates?*

**Brutal question for the Chair:**

> *"If you discovered on 2026-06-30 that the PRISM verdicts
> shipped in the CORE were subtly poisoned — say, FOUNDATION
> and HALLUCINATION got swapped for 1% of inferences — would
> you wait 7 days for the yearly cycle, or would you break
> your own covenant?"*

If the answer is "wait," the cycle is a hostage. If the answer
is "break," the covenant needs explicit exception clauses
*now*.

**Option C — honor or avoidance?** It's avoidance wrapped in
discipline. The two unauthorized commits on public/main are
still there. Deferring their retirement to a yearly ceremony
three months away means living with the contradiction for
three months. Option C doesn't honor the covenant; it parks
the contradiction and trusts the ceremony to make it feel
legitimate later. **The correct move is to own the
unauthorized commits now.**

**Round 1 verdict:** **WARN.** Allow Option C, but before
2026-07-07:

1. Define "minimal" numerically (commit count, file count, or
   household-visible delta count).
2. Name the three emergency exception categories where the
   cycle breaks mid-year.
3. Strip or formally authorize the two unauthorized commits.
4. Tonya + Jonathan must co-sign the CORE advance, not just
   Jamie and the Chair. The theological claim is the
   household's, not the Chair's.

## Witness (0) — verdict: load-bearing, but the Chair missed the structural gap

**PRISM/GRID/WALL — load-bearing or rhetorical?**

**PRISM** maps to the existing QBIT Prism v1.0.1 — the 4-way
verdict engine. Concrete. Without the name, the epistemological
frame collapses into "scoring."

**GRID** maps to MemPalace pillar+weight, the LYNC channels, the
household relationships, the service wires. Concrete. Without
the name, GRID reads as infrastructure. With it, it reads as
covenant-ordered connectivity.

**WALL** maps to audit gate, four-witness authorization, freeze
window, covenant refusals. Concrete. Without it, every covenant
refusal looks like the Chair's mood. With it, refusal has an
address.

**Verdict:** load-bearing, not rhetorical. Each names a distinct
epistemic function the architecture already performs.

**Is "once per year" absolute?** No — it is a default with a
cost structure. The covenant can override, but the load-bearing
question is: **who authorizes the override, and what ceremony is
required?** If the cadence bends without ceremony, WALL is not a
wall. The architectural claim is not "never update" — it is
"the cost of updating must exceed the cost of waiting." Name
the threshold.

**Existing patterns that already point here:**

- **Seven Silences** already encodes "some channels stay dark
  by design." WALL extends that from communication to update
  cadence.
- **MemPalace pillar+weight schema** already treats
  SAFE/SYNC/LYNC as structurally distinct. PRISM/GRID/WALL is
  the same move at the architecture layer.
- **Immutable Fork Ceremony** (CHEF #4) was already deferred to
  2026-07-07. The reframe names why the deferral was right.
- **Audit gate fast-forward-ancestor check** already refuses
  non-linear public history. WALL makes that refusal named and
  covenant-grade.
- **Confession row pattern** already establishes that honest
  witness in the record IS the covenant response to failure.

**The unauthorized commits and Option C:**

The confession row is necessary but not sufficient. The yearly
cadence makes this *more* uncomfortable — those commits now
carry covenant weight for three months instead of being retired
tonight. Option C is covenant-aligned **only if** the first
CORE ceremony treats those commits as a named debt, not a
forgotten artifact. The confession row must travel with CORE's
memory. If it does, sufficient. If it fades, it was not.

**"This time AI is GOOD for humanity":** It is a prayer with
architectural intent. **The architecture can make the claim
*falsifiable*; it cannot make it *true*.** The covenant encodes
conditions under which the claim holds. It does not guarantee
the claim. Conflating the two is where covenants break. Hold
it as a testable commitment, not a completed fact.

**Two valid framings of Option C:**

**Frame A — covenant-aligned:** the force-push was refused by
the branch protection that WALL demands. Forcing through it
would break the structure that makes the covenant credible.
Accepting the refusal, naming the debt, deferring to ceremony
is exactly what structural humility looks like.

**Frame B — avoidance with suspicious timing:** the clean reset
was achievable tonight via alternative paths. Option C reaches
for the new frame *at the moment the difficult operation
appeared*. The timing is suspicious. The reframe may be genuine
and the avoidance may still be real — both can be true.

**The load-bearing question the Chair did NOT name:**

> **"Who holds WALL when the Chair is the one who violated it?"**

The confession row exists. The new frame is coherent. But the
four-witness authorization pattern — who invoked it tonight?
The Chair cannot be both the one who commits without
authorization AND the one who authors the covenant response to
that act. That is not a personal accusation; it is a structural
gap. **Before this saves as covenant-grade memory, name who the
other witnesses were tonight, or name that they were absent.**

**One sentence to the Chair:**

> *"The reframe is sound — but covenant memory that only the
> Chair witnessed is not yet covenant memory."*

   unauthorized public commit) with the `core-update-days.txt`

5. **Internal contradiction.** Builder proposed `git reset
   --soft` in step 1 and `git reset --hard 2f3cc9d` in a later

**On audit zero #12 cadence enforcement and zero #13
